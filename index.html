My story

How I got here

Ever since I was a kid, I've been fascinated by how things work. I'd take pens apart in class to see how the click mechanism worked, open up electronics just to see what was inside. That same curiosity brought me to software, where I get to explore systems, understand how pieces fit together, and build solutions from the inside out.

I got into coding because I wanted to build tools for my basketball community. What started as simple team management scripts turned into full-stack applications serving hundreds of women athletes. Today, I focus on creating products that actually help people—whether that's streamlining league operations or supporting job seekers through career transitions.

My work is about clarity, usability, and impact. I build tools that bring communities together and help people get things done.

My little joys: I love getting outside—hiking, playing basketball, or just spending time with friends and family. It keeps me energized and inspired for the work I do.

How I work

I build tools that solve real problems for businesses. Here's my approach:

Start with the problem

We begin by understanding what you're trying to solve or achieve. What's working now? What's not? What would make your business run better or serve your customers more effectively?

Build for clarity and usability

Whether it's a website or a full application, I focus on making things clear and easy to use—both for you and your customers. Complicated tools don't help anyone.

Keep it maintainable

I build with clean code and good practices so your project is solid now and can grow with you later. You shouldn't be stuck with something you can't update or expand.
